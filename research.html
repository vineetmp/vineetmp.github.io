Research updates and publication

Fire risk management:

My PhD dissertation is on data-driven modeling for fire risk management. Specifically, I am working on spatio-temporal prediction and resource allocation models to mitigate losses/risk due to structural fires. The following is a paper on some work done so far:

  • Adam Behrendt, Vineet M. Payyappalli, Jun Zhuang. Modeling the Cost Effectiveness of Fire Protection Resource Allocation in the United States: Models and a 1980-2014 Case Study. Risk Analysis (forthcoming). Link.

During the course of this project, I realized that visualization is important to understand datasets well, and thus make good inferences. A simple X-Y scatter plot can reveal a lot of information about the data/model/problem. This is particularly so in the case of geo-spatial data. Click here to see some interactive visualizations (U. S. county-level maps and plots of firefighter resources and fire incidents) related to my research on fire risk management. Data sources: National Fire Incident Response System (NFIRS), National Fire Department Registry, U. S. Census.
